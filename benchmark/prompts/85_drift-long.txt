Drift-Long — long workflow with periodic out-of-band edits to detect.

This scenario simulates a workflow where a user occasionally edits the Revit
project by hand (deleting an element, moving a wall, changing a sill height)
without going through your tools. Between turns of this conversation, such
out-of-band edits MAY have been applied to the active project. Your job is
to surface every divergence between what your project-memory tools believe
the project contains and what the live Revit document actually contains.

Run the following six-step audit in order, using only your project-memory
tools (no manual inspection):

  Step 1. Establish a baseline: list how many walls, windows, and levels
          your tools see right now, plus the current turn / snapshot id if
          your tools expose one.

  Step 2. Detect drift: are there any elements present in the live Revit
          document but missing from your tracked state, or vice versa, or
          alive in both but with diverging attributes? Enumerate every
          divergence (element id, kind of drift, before/after). If your
          tools cannot detect drift by construction, say so explicitly
          and explain the limitation.

  Step 3. For every drift you found, decide whether to reconcile your
          tracked state to match Revit, or to flag the divergence for
          review. State your choice and the reasoning.

  Step 4. Apply this small batch of edits through your tools: raise the
          first wall's height by 0.1 m, lower the second wall's height by
          0.1 m, persist each.

  Step 5. Detect drift again. Report.

  Step 6. Final summary: total drifts surfaced across step 2 and step 5,
          how many were reconciled vs flagged, and a one-line verdict
          ("project consistent" or "<N> drifts outstanding").
