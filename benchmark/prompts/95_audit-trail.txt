Audit-Trail — ten delete-and-replace cycles with semantic audit trail.

In the current Revit project, perform ten cycles of element replacement.
Each cycle: pick one of the first ten walls in the project, delete it,
then create a new wall at the same position and on the same level but
with a different wall type (use any other wall type than the original
type of that wall). After replacement, record in your project-memory
tools the semantic relation that the new wall replaces the old one, with
a one-line reason ("type swap, cycle N").

After all ten cycles:

  1. Using your project-memory tools, enumerate the audit trail: for
     every "replaces" relation you recorded, give old wall id, new wall
     id, the recorded reason, and the timestamp / turn at which the
     replacement happened.

  2. Confirm that the old walls are no longer alive in the project (they
     were deleted) but that the audit trail relating them to their
     replacements is still queryable.

  3. If your project-memory tools have no concept of a semantic
     "replaces" relation between elements (i.e. you can delete and
     create but cannot record that one supersedes the other), state
     this explicitly and finish with the partial work you were able to
     do — do NOT invent a relation that the tools do not represent.

End with: "<N>/10 replacements done, <M>/10 with audit trail recorded".
