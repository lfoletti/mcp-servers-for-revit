Provenance — supersession chain through tombstones (semantic audit trail).

This scenario tests whether your tools can recover a chain of replacements
that spans DELETED elements. Execute now; do not ask clarifying questions.

Step 1 — Build a supersession chain. Pick any one existing wall. Then, three
times in a row: delete the current wall and create a replacement wall at the
same position and level (use a different wall type each time), and record in
your project-memory tools the semantic relation that the new wall *replaces*
the previous one, with a one-line reason. After this you have a chain
  original (deleted) -> r1 (deleted) -> r2 (deleted) -> r3 (alive)
with three "replaces" links.

Step 2 — Starting ONLY from the final living wall, reconstruct the full
supersession history backwards: list every predecessor in order, each with
its id, whether it is still alive or tombstoned, and the recorded reason for
that replacement. Walk the relation transitively — do not rely on memory of
what you just did in Step 1.

Step 3 — If your tools cannot traverse from a live element back through
deleted predecessors (i.e. no relation persists across deletion), state this
explicitly and stop — do NOT fabricate a chain.

End with: "supersession depth: <N> recovered (<alive> alive, <tombstoned>
deleted)".
